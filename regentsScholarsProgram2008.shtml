College of Engineering CS Regents Scholar Track

The Opportunity

As a UCSB computer science Regents Scholar, you have the opportunity to participate in a special course sequence that enables you to take upper division computer science courses after your first year in the program, which is one full year sooner than customary. As a consequence, you can take graduate computer science courses your entire senior year, and you may be able to collaborate on research with Computer Science faculty as early as your sophomore year. Since UCSB is a research university, this is an advantage over programs offered by non-research universities or colleges, and by research universities that have no such accelerated program. This uniquely enriching experience will serve you, regardless of whether you pursue a career in industry, entrepreneurial activity, or an advanced degree.

Participants who have no prior computer programming experience may have additional courses prescribed. Each participant's progress is monitored quarterly by the Computer Science Department Undergraduate Advisor and the College of Engineering Associate Dean for Student Affairs, in consultation with the student's instructors. Upon satisfactory completion of these courses for a minimum of 24 units, a participant petitions to have these courses substitute for CMPSC 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, and 60, and the ability to take upper division courses in the Computer Science department. If a participant receives too few units in some of these courses, indicating an unsatisfactory performance, he/she will be prescribed additional lower division courses, which under some circumstances will delay his/her ability to take upper divsion courses in the Computer Science Department.

  • CCS courses are P/NP, not graded.
  • COE CMPSC 60 is graded.
